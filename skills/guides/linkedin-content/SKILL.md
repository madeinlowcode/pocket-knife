# LinkedIn Content Strategy

## Why LinkedIn Is Different

LinkedIn rewards **dwell time** (how long someone reads your post) and **meaningful comments** over passive likes. The algorithm favors posts that generate back-and-forth conversation within the first 60–90 minutes of publishing.

---

## Post Types and When to Use Each

### 1. Text-Only Post (2200 chars max)
**Best for:** Personal stories, opinions, lessons learned
**Algorithm strength:** Highest organic reach per type
**Structure:**
```
Line 1: Hook (make them click "see more")
[blank line]
Lines 2–5: Setup / context
[blank line]
Lines 6–10: Core insight or story
[blank line]
Lines 11–13: Takeaway or lesson
[blank line]
Line 14: Question or CTA
[3–5 hashtags]
```

### 2. Image Post
**Best for:** Data visualizations, quote cards, before/after, screenshots
**Tips:**
- Text-on-image should be readable at thumbnail size
- First image is the hook — make it visually provocative
- Add image alt text (accessibility + SEO)

### 3. Carousel (PDF upload)
**Best for:** Step-by-step guides, frameworks, lists, case studies
**Structure:** See the carousel skill guide for full detail
**LinkedIn-specific:** PDFs upload as carousels. 10–15 slides optimal.

### 4. Video Post
**Best for:** Thought leadership, demos, talking-head takes
**Specs:** Square (1:1) or vertical (4:5) performs better than landscape
**First 3 seconds must hook without sound** (85% watch muted)

### 5. Article (LinkedIn Publishing)
**Best for:** SEO-driven long-form, comprehensive guides, research
**Note:** Lower immediate reach than posts but long-tail discoverability
**Length sweet spot:** 1200–2000 words

### 6. Poll
**Best for:** Engagement, market research, starting debate
**Tip:** Frame options so there is no "right" answer — controversy drives participation

## Hook Formulas (First Line)

The first line must survive the "see more" cut. Never start with:
- "I am thrilled/excited/honored to announce..."
- "Great news!"
- "This is a long post but..."

**Proven Hook Structures:**

```
Contrarian:
"[Common belief] is the reason most [audience] stay stuck."

Numbered promise:
"[X] things nobody tells you about [topic]:"

Story opener:
"[Time period] ago, I [specific situation]. Today, [result]."

Provocative question:
"What if everything you know about [topic] is backwards?"

Bold claim:
"[Specific claim]. And I have the data to prove it."

Vulnerability:
"I made a [specific mistake]. Here's what it cost me — and what I learned."
```

---

## Storytelling Framework (SPEAR)

Use for personal post narratives:

- **S — Situation:** Set the scene. Be specific (year, role, company stage).
- **P — Problem:** What was broken, hard, or at stake?
- **E — Escalation:** What made it worse before it got better?
- **A — Action:** What did you do (or decide) differently?
- **R — Result + Reflection:** Outcome + lesson the reader can apply.

## Hashtag Strategy for LinkedIn

- Use **3–5 hashtags maximum** — more looks spammy to the algorithm
- Place hashtags at the **end** of the post, not mid-text
- Mix one broad (#leadership, #marketing) + two niche (#b2bsaas, #productgrowth) + one branded
- Check hashtag follower count: target 10K–500K followers per hashtag
- Avoid banned or overused hashtags (#follow, #like, #success used as filler)

## Algorithm Tips

**Post timing:**
- Best: Tuesday–Thursday, 7–8 AM or 12–1 PM (audience local time)
- Avoid: Friday afternoon, weekends (B2B audiences are offline)

**First 60 minutes matter most:**
- Respond to every comment within the first hour
- Ask a question in your post to seed comment volume
- Notify 3–5 connections who would genuinely find it valuable

**Do not:**
- Edit the post in the first 30 minutes (resets distribution)
- Include external links in the post body (LinkedIn suppresses these)
  - Instead: Put links in the first comment and reference it in the post

**Do:**
- Tag people only if they are directly relevant (not spam-tagging)
- Use line breaks liberally — walls of text kill mobile readability
- Post consistently: 3–5x per week beats sporadic viral attempts

## Engagement Pod Guidelines

**What they are:** Small groups (5–20 people) who commit to commenting on each other's posts quickly after publishing.

**Best practices:**
- Keep groups under 15 people
- Comments must be substantive (4+ words, relevant to content)
- Rotate so the same names don't always appear first
- Niche pods (same industry/role) drive more relevant reach

**Avoid:** Automated pod tools — LinkedIn detects and penalizes coordinated inauthentic behavior.

## Content Calendar for LinkedIn

```
Week Structure:
Mon → Industry insight or hot take (text post)
Wed → Educational carousel or framework
Fri → Personal story or vulnerability post

Monthly:
- 1 long-form article
- 1–2 polls for market research
- Comment on 10 posts/day (outbound engagement)
```

## Profile Optimization Checklist (Context for Content)

- [ ] Headline: Role + outcome you deliver (not just job title)
- [ ] Banner image: Reinforces your content positioning
- [ ] About section: Written in first person, ends with CTA
- [ ] Featured section: Showcases your best-performing post or lead magnet
- [ ] Creator Mode: Enabled (adds Follow button, unlocks newsletter feature)

---

## Post Audit Checklist

- [ ] Hook survives the "see more" cut on mobile
- [ ] No external link in the post body
- [ ] Line breaks every 1–3 lines for scannability
- [ ] Ends with a genuine question or specific CTA
- [ ] 3–5 hashtags at the end, not inline
- [ ] Tagged people are relevant and will likely engage
- [ ] Scheduled for Tuesday–Thursday morning
- [ ] First comment staged with any relevant link or additional resource
