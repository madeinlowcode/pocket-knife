# Press Release Writing Guide

## What a Press Release Is (and Isn't)
A press release is a formal announcement distributed to journalists and media outlets. It is written in journalistic style so reporters can extract what they need quickly — or in some cases, publish directly with minimal editing. It is not a marketing piece, a blog post, or an ad. Promotional language kills credibility.

---

## Inverted Pyramid Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│     MOST IMPORTANT INFO         │  ← Lead (who, what, when, where, why)
│     (answer all 5 Ws here)      │
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│   SUPPORTING DETAILS            │  ← Context, background, quotes
│   (expands on the lead)         │
├─────────────────────────────────┤
│  BACKGROUND / BOILERPLATE       │  ← Company description, additional context
│  (least urgent — cut if needed) │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why inverted pyramid?** Editors cut from the bottom. Readers stop at any point. The most critical facts must appear first — every time.

## Document Structure

### Standard Layout
```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE                          [or: EMBARGOED UNTIL: Date/Time]

HEADLINE
SUBHEADLINE (optional)

CITY, STATE, Month Day, Year — Lead paragraph...

Body paragraph 1...

Body paragraph 2 (quote)...

Body paragraph 3...

Second quote paragraph...

Boilerplate ("About [Company]")...

###   [indicates end of release]

MEDIA CONTACT:
Name
Title
Company
Phone
Email
```

## Headline Formula

### The Headline Must Do Three Things
1. State what happened
2. Imply why it matters
3. Use the company or product name

### Headline Formulas
**[Company] [Verb] [Specific Thing] [Context/Impact]**

Examples:
- "Acme Corp Launches AI-Powered Fraud Detection for Community Banks"
- "Brightfield Raises $22M Series B to Expand Workforce Intelligence Platform"
- "MedTrack Earns FDA Clearance for Remote Patient Monitoring Device"

**Rules:**
- Headline case: Title Case (capitalize all major words)
- Maximum 100–120 characters
- Do not start with the company name alone — lead with the news
- No exclamation points
- No superlatives ("best," "leading," "revolutionary") — these are marketing language
- Headline is in present tense ("Launches," "Raises," "Announces")

### Subheadline
- Adds one layer of specificity not in the headline
- Sentence case (only first word and proper nouns capitalized)
- Maximum 160 characters
- Written in present or future tense

## Lead Paragraph

### The Lead Formula
```
[Company name], [brief description], today [announced/launched/released] [what],
[context or significance], [date/location if relevant].
```

Example:
> "Acme Corp, a provider of AI fraud detection software for financial institutions, today launched FraudGuard 3.0, its most advanced detection platform to date, reducing false positive rates by 40% for banks under $5 billion in assets."

### Lead Rules
- One paragraph, maximum 40–50 words
- Must contain: who, what, when, where, why
- No jargon or acronyms in the lead — define later if needed
- Write it so that if a journalist only reads this one paragraph, they understand the story

## Body Paragraphs

### Paragraph 2: Significance and Context
Expand on the "why it matters." Provide market context, problem being solved, or timing significance.

- 2–3 sentences
- Include a data point if available (market size, customer count, growth metric)
- No bullet points in the body — press releases use paragraphs

### Paragraph 3: First Quote
**Quote rules:**
- Attribute to a specific named executive (CEO, CTO, or most relevant leader)
- The quote should say something only that person can say — perspective, opinion, strategy
- Never use a quote to state a fact ("Our product has 500 features" is not a quote)
- Never use hollow enthusiasm ("We are thrilled and excited to announce...")
- Quotes are in past tense when reporting ("said"), present tense when the quote itself speaks

**Weak quote:**
> "We are really excited about this launch," said Jane Smith, CEO of Acme Corp. "We think it's going to be a great product for our customers."

**Strong quote:**
> "Community banks have been underserved by enterprise fraud tools that weren't built for their transaction volumes," said Jane Smith, CEO of Acme Corp. "FraudGuard 3.0 changes that equation — and the 40% reduction in false positives means compliance teams can focus on real threats."

### Paragraph 4: Product/Announcement Details
Specifics of what is being announced. Features, pricing, availability, partnerships, metrics.

### Paragraph 5: Second Quote (optional)
Customer, partner, or industry analyst quote adds third-party credibility.

## Boilerplate ("About" Section)

Every press release ends with a standard company description. This is the boilerplate.

### Boilerplate Template
```
About [Company Name]
[Company Name] [what the company does, one sentence]. [Founded/established year],
[company] serves [customer type] across [geography/markets].
[Key differentiator or proof point — customer count, revenue, recognition].
For more information, visit [website].
```

**Boilerplate rules:**
- 50–80 words maximum
- Written in third person
- Factual — no marketing language
- Must include website URL
- Keep it updated — review quarterly

## AP Style Basics for Press Releases

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above
- Exception: Always use numerals for ages, percentages, money, measurements
- Percentages: Use numerals + "percent" (not the % symbol) in running text
- Money: $5 million (not $5,000,000 in running text)
- $1.2 billion, $750,000 — no abbreviations like "K" or "M" in formal text

### Dates and Times
- Month abbreviations: Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec. (March, April, May, June, July are never abbreviated)
- Date format: January 15, 2025 (no ordinals — not "January 15th")
- Time: 10 a.m., 2:30 p.m. (lowercase, with periods)
- Time zones: EST, PST, CST (no periods in time zone abbreviations)

### Titles
- Formal titles before a name are capitalized: Chief Executive Officer Jane Smith
- Titles after a name are lowercase: Jane Smith, chief executive officer
- Long titles before names use a comma: Jane Smith, chief executive officer of Acme Corp, said...

### Company Names
- Use the company's official capitalization (e.g., eBay, iPhone, GitHub)
- Do not add "Inc." or "Corp." after the first reference unless the full legal name is required

### Quotation Marks
- Periods and commas go inside quotation marks
- "The product is ready," said Smith. (period inside)

## Distribution Checklist

### Before Sending
- [ ] Embargo instructions or "For Immediate Release" at the top
- [ ] Headline is factual, not promotional
- [ ] Lead paragraph answers all five Ws in under 50 words
- [ ] At least one named quote from a company executive
- [ ] Quote expresses perspective — not a restatement of facts
- [ ] Boilerplate is current and accurate
- [ ] "###" end marker present
- [ ] Media contact information complete (name, phone, email)
- [ ] All claims are verifiable and approved
- [ ] Wire service formatting requirements met (if using PR Newswire, BusinessWire, etc.)

### Distribution Targets
- Industry trade publications (most relevant for product launches)
- Local business press (for funding, hiring, local expansion)
- National business press (for significant funding rounds, major partnerships)
- Direct journalist outreach (personalized pitch email accompanies the release)
- Company newsroom / blog (publish the release as a page on your website)
