# Product Hunt Launch Playbook

## How Product Hunt Works

Products are listed and voted on within a 24-hour window (12:01 AM PST to midnight PST). The leaderboard resets daily. Top 5 products of the day get featured in the daily digest email to 500K+ subscribers. Top product of the week gets additional newsletter placement.

Votes from early in the day carry more algorithmic weight. The first 4 hours after midnight PST are critical.

---

## Pre-Launch Timeline

### 4–6 Weeks Before Launch

- [ ] Create your Product Hunt account (or verify it's active)
- [ ] Start engaging with PH: upvote other products, comment genuinely
- [ ] Research hunters: find active hunters aligned with your category
- [ ] Build your upcoming page on PH to collect "notify me" subscribers
- [ ] Finalize tagline (5–7 words, benefit-forward)
- [ ] Draft your full description (250–300 words)
- [ ] Identify your launch date (Tuesday–Thursday perform best)

### 2–3 Weeks Before Launch

- [ ] Contact your chosen hunter and confirm they will submit the product
- [ ] Prepare all assets (thumbnail, gallery images, video demo)
- [ ] Draft the first comment (crucial — see section below)
- [ ] Build your launch support list: who will you notify at 12:01 AM PST?
- [ ] Schedule social posts across your channels
- [ ] Brief your team, investors, and community

### 1 Week Before Launch

- [ ] Final asset review: all images at correct dimensions
- [ ] Test your product link works for a first-time visitor
- [ ] Prepare email drafts for your subscriber list
- [ ] Set your personal alarm for 12:01 AM PST

### Day Before Launch

- [ ] Confirm hunter is ready and has all assets
- [ ] Pre-write all response templates for common comments
- [ ] Load launch day email and social media queue
- [ ] Brief support team to expect volume

## Tagline Formula

The tagline is 5–7 words that appear under your product name. It is the first thing people read.

**Structure:** [What it does] + [Who it's for] + [Key differentiator]

```
Good examples:
"AI writing tool for solo founders"
"Turn meetings into action items instantly"
"The CRM that writes your follow-ups"

Bad examples:
"The future of productivity" (vague)
"A platform for seamless collaboration" (generic)
"Build, ship, grow" (meaningless without context)
```

Test your tagline: can someone who has never heard of your product understand what it does in 3 seconds?

## Asset Specifications

| Asset | Dimensions | Format | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Thumbnail (logo) | 240 x 240 px | PNG | Clean, no text if possible |
| Gallery images | 1270 x 760 px | PNG/JPG | Up to 5 images + 1 video |
| Demo video | 1270 x 760 px | MP4/GIF | Under 3 minutes, no sound required |

### Gallery Image Strategy

1. **Hero shot** — Product in context, showing the core action
2. **Feature highlight 1** — Your biggest differentiator, labeled
3. **Feature highlight 2** — Second key feature or use case
4. **Social proof** — Customer quote, logo wall, or metric ("10,000 teams use X")
5. **How it works** — Simple 3-step flow diagram

## Hunter Selection

A hunter is the Product Hunt user who submits your product. They are not required to have used your product — they are a sponsor of the submission.

**What makes a good hunter:**
- Has a history of submitting products that performed well (check their profile)
- Has a following relevant to your category
- Is responsive and reliable (confirm commitment in writing)
- Is willing to share the launch with their audience

**Finding hunters:**
- Browse PH profiles of frequent submitters in your category
- Search "Product Hunt hunters" on Twitter/X to find active community members
- Tools: phunter.co, hunter.cat

**Alternatives:** Founders can self-submit. Losing a hunter is not fatal — your community support matters more than the hunter's follower count.

## The First Comment

The first comment is posted by you (the maker) immediately after launch goes live. It is the most-read piece of content on your listing.

**Structure:**

```
Paragraph 1 — Origin story (2–3 sentences)
Why did you build this? What problem did you personally face?

Paragraph 2 — What it does (2–3 sentences)
Explain clearly. Assume no prior context. Name the core use case.

Paragraph 3 — Who it's for (1–2 sentences)
Be specific about your target user.

Paragraph 4 — What's different (1–2 sentences)
Your key differentiator vs. alternatives. Be honest, not arrogant.

Paragraph 5 — Ask for feedback (1 sentence)
"We'd love your honest feedback — especially on [specific thing]."
```

**Tips:**
- Keep it under 300 words
- No hype words ("game-changer", "revolutionary", "disrupting")
- Personal tone — write as a founder, not as a press release
- Post this within 2 minutes of launch going live (set a timer)

## Launch Day Communication

### Outreach Sequence (12:01 AM PST onward)

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| 12:01 AM | Hunter submits; post first comment immediately |
| 12:05 AM | Send personal Slack/Discord/email to inner circle (10–20 people) |
| 7:00 AM PST | Send email newsletter to subscriber list |
| 9:00 AM PST | Post on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram |
| 12:00 PM PST | Progress update post ("We're #X on PH today, thank you!") |
| 3:00 PM PST | Reply to all PH comments you haven't answered yet |
| 6:00 PM PST | Final push post to community channels |
| 11:00 PM PST | Thank you post if you made top 5 |

### Community Outreach Rules

- Ask for support personally, not with mass-blast copy-paste messages
- Provide the direct PH link in every message
- Do NOT ask people to "upvote" — PH penalizes coordinated upvoting patterns
- Ask: "Would you check it out and leave honest feedback?" (Votes follow naturally)

---

## Follow-Up (Day After Launch)

- [ ] Post a recap: how it went, what you learned, key metrics
- [ ] Reply to every remaining PH comment
- [ ] Send a thank-you email to your subscriber list
- [ ] Amplify positive PH reviews to other channels
- [ ] Add "Featured on Product Hunt" badge to your website
- [ ] Archive the PH listing URL for future social proof

---

## Launch Day Checklist

**Before midnight PST:**
- [ ] Hunter confirmed and ready
- [ ] All assets uploaded and verified in the draft listing
- [ ] First comment drafted and saved
- [ ] Outreach list finalized
- [ ] Social posts scheduled for 7–9 AM PST
- [ ] Email ready to send at 7 AM PST

**After launch:**
- [ ] First comment posted within 2 minutes
- [ ] Inner circle notified personally
- [ ] All PH comments monitored and replied to every 2 hours
- [ ] Progress updates posted mid-day
- [ ] Team celebrating (or sleeping — it's 12 AM PST)
