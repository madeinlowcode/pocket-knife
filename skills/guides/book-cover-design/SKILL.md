# Book Cover Design Guide

## Core Purpose

A book cover must do three jobs simultaneously: signal genre immediately, create emotional resonance, and make the title/author legible at thumbnail size. A cover that fails any one of these jobs loses sales.

---

## Genre Convention Reference

Genre conventions are not limitations — they are the shared visual language readers use to self-select. Breaking them requires a deliberate, calculated reason.

### Literary Fiction
- Restrained, symbolic imagery
- Serif or high-craft display fonts
- Muted, sophisticated palettes
- Texture and paper grain often applied
- Author name may be equal to or larger than title

### Commercial Thriller / Crime
- High contrast, often dark
- Strong directional lighting or silhouette
- Sans-serif or condensed bold type
- Single dominant image (figure, location, object)
- Foil, emboss, or spot UV on print

### Romance
- **Contemporary**: Warm colors, couples or single person, soft focus
- **Historical**: Period costume, painterly illustration
- **Paranormal**: Dark palette, supernatural elements, often male figure
- Couple or suggestive cover remains standard in mass market

### Fantasy / Sci-Fi
- **Epic Fantasy**: Illustrated landscapes, armor, magic systems
- **Urban Fantasy**: Gritty cityscape, stylized character
- **Sci-Fi**: Clean geometry, space vistas, technological elements
- Hand-lettered or custom display type is common and expected

### Self-Help / Business
- Bold, simple, high-contrast
- Author photo on back or inside — not usually front
- Abstract geometric or photographic metaphor
- Clean sans-serif typography at large scale

### Children's / Middle Grade
- Illustration-forward
- Expressive characters with visible emotion
- Bright, saturated palette
- Playful, rounded letterforms

## Typography Rules

### Front Cover Type Hierarchy
1. **Title** — largest, most distinctive
2. **Subtitle** (if present) — 40–60% of title size
3. **Author name** — varies by fame; debut authors smaller, established equal or larger

### Font Selection Principles
- Use maximum 2 typefaces on the front cover
- One expressive/display font, one clean supporting font
- Script fonts: use sparingly, only for title or decorative accent
- Test at 100px wide — if unreadable, reconsider the font
- Avoid default system fonts (Arial, Times New Roman) — they signal amateur work

### Common Typography Pairings by Genre
- Literary: Caslon + Futura | Garamond + Gill Sans
- Thriller: Bebas Neue + Source Sans | Impact + Helvetica
- Romance: Playfair Display + Lato | Bodoni + Raleway
- Business: Proxima Nova + Minion | Montserrat + Freight Text

### Legibility at Thumbnail
- At Amazon thumbnail size (approximately 500 x 800 px), only the title should be readable
- Test by reducing your design to 100px wide — title must be legible
- Avoid titles that require more than 2 lines at thumbnail scale

## Color Psychology

### Color Roles on a Cover
- **Dominant color**: Sets genre mood and shelf appeal (60%)
- **Secondary color**: Creates contrast and depth (30%)
- **Accent color**: Draws eye to title or key element (10%)

### Color by Emotional Intent
- **Red**: Urgency, passion, danger, energy — thriller, romance, horror
- **Blue**: Trust, calm, intelligence, distance — business, sci-fi, literary
- **Black**: Power, mystery, sophistication, death — noir, thriller, horror
- **Green**: Growth, nature, hope, envy — self-help, fantasy, literary
- **Gold/Yellow**: Wealth, optimism, caution — business, fantasy, romance
- **White**: Clarity, minimalism, clinical — literary fiction, non-fiction
- **Purple**: Luxury, magic, spirituality — fantasy, paranormal, spiritual

### Practical Color Rules
- Dark covers recede on screen, bright covers pop — know your channel
- Avoid pure black (#000000) as dominant — use very dark navy or charcoal
- Warm covers feel inviting; cool covers feel authoritative
- Test your palette against bestseller covers in your genre before finalizing

## Imagery and Composition

### Focal Point Hierarchy
1. Single dominant focal point (subject, scene, or object)
2. Supporting visual layer (background, texture)
3. Type layer (integrates with, not fights, imagery)

### Photography vs. Illustration
- Photography: faster, more realistic, better for contemporary settings
- Illustration: more distinctive, genre-appropriate for fantasy/romance, expensive
- Compositing (photo + texture + graphic): most common professional approach

### Composition Approaches
- **Rule of Thirds**: Subject off-center, creates natural space for title
- **Symmetrical/centered**: Works for literary fiction, commands attention
- **Figure from below**: Creates heroic, epic feel — common in thriller/fantasy
- **Wide establishing shot**: Creates mood and setting — common in literary

## Spine Design

The spine sells the physical book on a shelf. It is often the only visible surface.

### Spine Checklist
- [ ] Title readable at 1" height (typical spine width for 250–350 pg book)
- [ ] Author name readable
- [ ] Publisher logo/imprint at bottom (if applicable)
- [ ] Spine color coordinates with front cover
- [ ] Text runs bottom-to-top (standard in English-language publishing)
- [ ] Type does not touch spine edges (minimum 3mm clear zone)

### Spine Width Calculation
- Pages ÷ pages-per-inch of paper stock = spine width
- Standard trade paper (60#): approximately 435 pages per inch
- Heavy paper (70#): approximately 360 pages per inch
- Always add 0.0625" bleed to each side of spine

## Back Cover Layout

### Standard Back Cover Structure (top to bottom)
1. **Tagline or hook** (optional but powerful): 1–2 lines
2. **Book description**: 100–200 words, first line is strongest
3. **Endorsement quote**: 1–2 sentences from credible source
4. **Author bio** (brief): 2–3 sentences, optional photo
5. **Publisher logo** + ISBN barcode (bottom right)
6. **Price** (if applicable, varies by market)

### Back Cover Typography
- Body text minimum 9pt for print
- Tagline 18–24pt
- All text at least 12.7mm (0.5") from trim edge
- Maintain same type family as front cover

---

## Print vs. Digital Considerations

### Print-Specific
- Work in CMYK, not RGB (unless printer accepts RGB)
- 300 DPI minimum for all raster elements
- Include 3mm bleed on all exterior edges
- Embed all fonts before export
- Check ink density: total ink should not exceed 280–300% (CMYK combined)

### Digital/eBook Specific
- RGB color mode
- 2560 x 1600 px recommended for Kindle/eBook
- 72–150 DPI acceptable (PPI matters more than DPI for screen)
- Ensure cover reads at 100px wide in search results
- No foil or emboss effects (irrelevant on screen)

---

## Checklist Before Finalizing

- [ ] Genre is identifiable in under 3 seconds
- [ ] Title is readable at thumbnail size
- [ ] Color contrast meets accessibility minimums
- [ ] No copyrighted images used without license
- [ ] Front, spine, and back design are visually unified
- [ ] All fonts are licensed for commercial use
- [ ] Print files exported at 300 DPI with bleed and crop marks
- [ ] Digital files at correct dimensions for all target platforms
- [ ] Compared against 5 current bestsellers in same genre
- [ ] Tested at thumbnail size on white and dark backgrounds
