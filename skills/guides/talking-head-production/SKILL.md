# Talking Head Video Production Guide

## What Defines a Great Talking Head Video
The viewer should forget they are watching a video. The speaker is credible, the visuals are clean, and the audio is effortless to listen to. Remove every distraction — technical or visual — that pulls attention from the message.

## Lighting Setup

### Three-Point Lighting (Standard Setup)
```
                        [KEY LIGHT]
                          /
         [FILL LIGHT] -- [SUBJECT] -- [BACK LIGHT]
```

**Key Light**
- Primary light source; positioned 45 degrees to the side and slightly above eye level
- Softbox or ring light at 2–3 feet from subject
- Should illuminate 60–70% of the face
- Color temperature: 5500–6000K (daylight) for modern/clean look; 3200K for warmer feel

**Fill Light**
- Reduces shadows from key light — placed on opposite side
- Intensity: 50% of key light (half as bright)
- If using a window as key light, a white reflector or foam board on the opposite side works as fill

**Back Light (Hair/Rim Light)**
- Placed behind the subject, pointing at the back of the head/shoulders
- Separates the subject from the background
- Creates professional depth; without it, subject "merges" into background
- Use a small LED panel at low intensity

### Natural Light Setup
- Position subject facing the window (not side-lit, not backlit)
- Diffuse harsh sunlight with a white sheer curtain or translucent panel
- Overcast days are ideal — giant natural softbox
- Avoid mixing daylight and tungsten — causes color inconsistency

### Common Lighting Mistakes
| Mistake                           | Result                          | Fix                                       |
|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Overhead ceiling light only       | Raccoon eyes, unflattering      | Add front-facing key light                |
| Subject backlit by window         | Silhouette, underexposed face   | Flip subject to face window               |
| Ring light at eye level, centered | Unnatural ring catchlight       | Raise ring light above eye level, tilt down |
| Mixed color temperatures          | Orange/blue skin tone conflict  | Match all lights to same Kelvin           |

## Framing (Rule of Thirds Applied)

### Camera Height
- Position camera lens at or very slightly above eye level
- Below eye level: subject looks dominant (avoid for educational/helpful content)
- Too far above eye level: subject looks submissive, neck appears compressed

### Horizontal Framing
- Subject's eyes sit on the upper third horizontal line
- Leave breathing room above the head (10–15% of frame)
- Avoid chopping at the chin, neck, or shoulders — frame at mid-chest minimum
- For interview feel: offset subject to one side, leave lead room in the direction they face

### Distance and Lens
- **Tight (MCU, CU)**: Intimate, confessional, educational
- **Medium (MS)**: Standard, conversational, balanced
- Avoid wide angle lenses close to the face — distorts facial features
- 50mm–85mm equivalent focal length: most flattering for faces

### Framing Checklist
- [ ] Eyes on upper-third line
- [ ] No objects appearing to grow from head
- [ ] Clean edge between subject and background
- [ ] Camera at eye level
- [ ] Subject framed with appropriate headroom

## Audio Quality

### Microphone Options (ranked by quality)
1. **Lavalier (lapel) mic**: Clips to clothing, consistent level, minimal handling noise
2. **Shotgun mic on camera**: Good if subject stays still, boom arm preferred
3. **Cardioid condenser on desk stand**: Good for seated, static presentation
4. **USB cardioid mic**: Budget-friendly, adequate for web content
5. **Built-in camera mic**: Avoid — reverb and distance kill credibility

### Acoustic Treatment
- Record in a room with soft furnishings (couch, carpet, curtains, bookshelves)
- Hard walls, tile, and glass create reverb — avoid or treat with foam panels
- A walk-in closet is the fastest improvised recording booth
- Hang a heavy blanket behind/around the microphone if no other option

### Audio Standards for Export
- Peak level: -3 dB maximum
- Target loudness: -16 LUFS (online streaming / social media)
- Remove background hum with a high-pass filter at 80–100 Hz
- Remove plosives (popping P/B sounds) with de-esser or pop filter on the mic

### On-Set Audio Checklist
- [ ] Microphone within 12–18 inches of mouth (lav) or 12 inches overhead (boom)
- [ ] Headphones used to monitor audio during recording
- [ ] Room tone recorded (10 sec silence) for post-production repair
- [ ] No HVAC, refrigerator hum, or street noise audible

## Background

### Background Options
| Background Type         | Best For                           | Avoid If                              |
|-------------------------|------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Blurred bokeh (large aperture) | Clean, cinematic, timeless | Subject moves significantly           |
| Solid color wall (painted) | Simple, brand-aligned           | Color clashes with skin tone or clothing |
| Bookshelf / office     | Authority, expertise, thought leadership | Messy, distracting items visible |
| Branded backdrop (fabric) | Consistent content series      | Budget-challenged setups              |
| Virtual background (software) | Remote/no dedicated space  | Requires green screen for quality     |
| Outdoor / lifestyle    | Authentic, casual content          | Uncontrolled wind noise, changing light |

### Background Depth Rule
- Minimum 4–6 feet between subject and background when using bokeh
- Closer to wall = less separation = more amateur look

## Teleprompter Tips

### Types
- **Hardware teleprompter**: Glass reflection + tablet; most professional; eliminates eye drift
- **Software on a second screen**: Scroll script on monitor behind or below camera; works for static setups
- **Phone teleprompter app**: (Teleprompter Premium, PromptSmart) — basic but portable

### Script Formatting for Teleprompter
- Font size: 40–60pt depending on distance
- Line spacing: 1.5–2x — easier to track
- All caps or sentence case — both work; choose one and be consistent
- Keep lines short — break sentences at natural breathing points
- Mark emphasis with [BOLD] and pauses with [PAUSE] or / symbols

### Delivery Tips to Avoid "Teleprompter Look"
- Practice reading while keeping eyebrow and face muscles relaxed
- Look at the camera lens, not the words — the lens is in the center of the reflection
- Vary your reading speed slightly — do not maintain perfectly constant pace
- Gesture naturally — movement masks the reading state
- Know the content well enough that you can ad-lib a sentence; dependency on exact words sounds robotic

## Editing Workflow

### Talking Head Edit Order
1. Select the best takes (mark in-points and out-points)
2. Assemble rough cut (all selected clips in sequence)
3. Remove dead air, long pauses, filler words (um, uh, like)
4. Apply jump cuts — do not use dissolves between talking head cuts
5. Add B-roll over jump cuts and key concept moments
6. Color grade (match color across all clips first, then stylize)
7. Mix audio (normalize, EQ, compress, de-noise)
8. Add lower thirds, text overlays, graphics
9. Add intro/outro if applicable
10. Export

### Jump Cut Best Practices
- Cut mid-sentence is acceptable if audio is clean
- Always cut on a consonant sound — not mid-vowel
- Disguise jump cuts with a 1-frame zoom (3–5% crop between cuts) for smooth feel
- B-roll is preferred over visible jump cuts for extended removed passages

### B-Roll Strategy
- Every major concept mentioned should have a visual reinforcement
- Shoot 3:1 ratio — for every 1 minute of final video, have 3 minutes of B-roll options
- B-roll sources: screen recordings, product shots, lifestyle shots, stock footage

### Text Overlay Standards
- Lower third: Speaker name + title in first 5 sec
- Key points: Short text on screen (max 5 words) when speaker says most important lines
- Font: Sans-serif, high contrast — legible at phone size
- Duration: Each text overlay on screen minimum 2 sec, maximum 5 sec

## Final Export Checklist
- [ ] Audio levels at -16 LUFS, no peaks above -3 dB
- [ ] Color consistent across all clips
- [ ] Captions/subtitles added (SRT or burned-in)
- [ ] Lower third with speaker name appears in first 10 sec
- [ ] No jump cuts without B-roll or zoom trick cover
- [ ] Safe zones respected for platform
- [ ] Exported as MP4, H.264, AAC audio, 1920×1080 minimum
