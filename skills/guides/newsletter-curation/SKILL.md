# Newsletter Curation Guide

## What Makes a Curated Newsletter Worth Subscribing To
Subscribers do not pay with money — they pay with attention and inbox space. The newsletter earns its place by doing something the reader cannot easily do themselves: filter the noise, add context, and deliver only what matters. Without a clear point of view, a curated newsletter is just a list of links.

---

## Defining Your Editorial Identity

### The Four Editorial Decisions (make these before writing issue #1)
1. **Topic domain**: What is the specific subject area? (Not "marketing" — "B2B demand generation for SaaS companies under 100 employees")
2. **Audience**: One specific reader type (job title, industry, stage of career, problem they have)
3. **Editorial lens**: What is your angle on the topic? (contrarian, practical, data-driven, global perspective, beginner-friendly)
4. **Frequency**: How often does it publish? (Commit to a schedule and keep it)

### Voice Calibration
| Voice Type         | Best For                                | Avoid                          |
|--------------------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| First-person expert | Thought leadership + curation        | When you lack genuine expertise |
| Neutral curator    | News aggregation, link roundups         | When readers want opinion      |
| Collaborative ("we") | Team newsletters, community voices  | Solo newsletters — feels odd   |
| Character/persona  | Entertainment-first newsletters         | B2B professional audiences     |

**Voice consistency rule**: Write every issue as if you are writing a message to one specific person — not a mass audience. Use "you" not "readers" or "subscribers."

## Content Sourcing Framework

### Source Types (use a mix)
- **RSS feeds**: Subscribe to 20–30 primary sources in your domain via Feedly or Inoreader
- **Twitter/X lists**: Curated follows of practitioners, researchers, journalists in your niche
- **Community monitoring**: Reddit, Slack groups, Discord, LinkedIn comments
- **Academic / research**: Google Scholar alerts, SSRN, pre-print servers for your domain
- **Newsletters you admire**: Meta-curation — but always add your own layer of commentary
- **Primary sources**: Earnings calls, product changelogs, regulatory filings (for business niches)
- **Listener/reader submissions**: Encourage replies and reader tips — builds community

### Weekly Sourcing Workflow
```
Day 1–5 (daily, 20–30 min): Scan RSS, save candidates to a read-later tool (Pocket, Instapaper, Notion)
Day 6 (45 min):             Read saved items, score each 1–3 (cut anything scored 1)
Day 7 (60 min):             Write annotations for items scored 2–3, finalize selection, draft issue
Send day:                   Proofread, schedule, send
```

### Scoring Criteria for Inclusion
Include an item if it satisfies at least 2 of 3:
1. Is it timely or newly relevant to the audience?
2. Does it contain a genuine insight or actionable takeaway?
3. Is it not already widely circulating in the mainstream press?

## Section Structure

### Standard Newsletter Architecture
```
1. SUBJECT LINE         (not a section — but determines open rate)
2. PREVIEW TEXT         (the 80–120 char snippet visible before opening)
3. INTRO / OPENER       (2–4 sentences: your editorial take on the week)
4. LEAD STORY           (the most important item; 100–200 words + link)
5. SUPPORTING ITEMS     (3–5 shorter items; 40–80 words each + link)
6. QUICK HITS           (optional; 5–10 one-liners with links; low-effort high-density)
7. ONE THING TO TRY     (optional; practical tip, tool, or resource)
8. CLOSING LINE         (1–2 sentences; personality, question, see-you-next-week)
9. CTA / SPONSOR UNIT   (if monetized; label clearly)
```

### Intro / Opener Rules
- Never start with "This week in..." — it is the most common opener and the least compelling
- Start with a provocative claim, an observation, or a question
- The opener should give a reason to read the whole issue — hint at the tension or theme
- Maximum 4 sentences — readers do not scroll down for a long intro

### Lead Story Annotation Template
```
[HEADLINE AS LINK]
What happened / what was published:   1 sentence
Why it matters to this audience:      1–2 sentences
Your take / what they should do:      1 sentence
```

### Quick Hits Format
- Each item: One sentence of context + link
- Use bold for the link text — scannable
- Group by sub-topic if possible
- Do not explain; just surface and label

## Subject Lines

### Subject Line Formula Types

**Curiosity gap**: "The metric no one is tracking in their newsletter"
**Direct value**: "5 ways to improve email open rates this week"
**News hook**: "Google changed how it indexes newsletters — here's what changed"
**Personal / conversational**: "I was wrong about subject lines"
**Data / specific number**: "47% of B2B emails are opened on mobile — does yours hold up?"
**List format**: "7 links worth your time this Friday"

### Subject Line Rules
- Under 50 characters for mobile optimization (ideally 30–40)
- Never use ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation (spam signals)
- Avoid "RE:" or "FWD:" tricks — erodes trust
- Preview text completes the subject line — they are one unit
- A/B test subject lines at 20% of list before sending to full list

### Preview Text
- 80–120 characters
- Should not repeat the subject line — it should extend it
- Treat it as the second half of a sentence the subject line started
- If not set, email clients pull the first line of email body — set it explicitly

## Send Frequency Guidelines

| Frequency       | Best For                                          | Risk                              |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Daily           | News-heavy niches (finance, tech news, crypto)    | Burnout for creator; churn if shallow |
| 3×/week         | Active communities with high content volume       | Hard to sustain quality           |
| Weekly          | Most niches; sweet spot for curation              | Miss news cycles                  |
| Biweekly        | Deeper-dive, lower-volume niches                  | Readers forget you exist          |
| Monthly         | Long-form, premium research, executive audiences  | Very low engagement surface area  |

**Send day and time:**
- B2B audiences: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10 AM local time consistently
- Consumer audiences: Saturday morning performs for weekend reading
- Consistency > optimization — send on the same day every week

## Growth Tactics

### Organic Growth
- **Referral program**: "Forward this to one person who'd love it — they can subscribe here" (link in every issue)
- **Twitter/LinkedIn thread**: Repurpose one key item from each issue as a native platform post; include subscribe link
- **Cross-promotions**: Partner with 2–3 newsletters in adjacent niches; swap recommendations
- **SEO landing page**: Optimize your subscribe page for "[niche] newsletter" search terms
- **Guest features**: Be featured in others' Quick Hits; feature others in yours

### Paid Growth
- Sponsor slots in newsletters with overlapping audiences
- Meta/LinkedIn lead gen ads targeting specific job titles with lead magnet
- Twitter/X follower campaigns targeting followers of key voices in your niche

### Retention
- Welcome email sequence (3-part): Issue 1 → Who I am + best past issues; Issue 2 → The format + how to get most value; Issue 3 → Ask for a reply
- Reply hooks: End each issue with one question — replies signal deliverability and build community
- Re-engagement campaign at 90 days inactive: "Still want these? Click to confirm."
- Unsubscribes are healthy — a smaller engaged list outperforms a large unengaged one

## Newsletter Quality Checklist

### Before Writing
- [ ] Source review complete, 5–8 items selected
- [ ] Items scored and cut to only the strongest
- [ ] Editorial angle or theme for this issue identified

### While Writing
- [ ] Opener does not start with "This week in..."
- [ ] Every item has a "why it matters" annotation, not just a summary
- [ ] Your voice and perspective is present — not neutral aggregation
- [ ] Subject line and preview text written as a pair

### Before Sending
- [ ] All links tested and verified
- [ ] Plain text version renders correctly
- [ ] Unsubscribe link present
- [ ] Preview text set (not auto-pulled from body)
- [ ] Sent to test address and checked on mobile
- [ ] Send time scheduled to audience's local timezone
