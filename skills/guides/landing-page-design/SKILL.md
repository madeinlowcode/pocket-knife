# Landing Page Design Guide

## Core Purpose

A landing page has one job: convert. Unlike a homepage (which serves many audiences), a landing page serves one visitor segment with one offer and one desired action. Every design decision must be evaluated against whether it helps or hinders that conversion.

---

## The Conversion Framework

### Visitor Psychology in Order
1. **What is this?** — Clarity of offer (0–3 seconds)
2. **Is this for me?** — Relevance and targeting (3–8 seconds)
3. **Why should I trust this?** — Credibility signals (8–15 seconds)
4. **What do I get?** — Value proposition depth (active reading)
5. **What do I risk?** — Objection handling
6. **What should I do?** — Clear next action

Every section of the page corresponds to one or more of these questions.

---

## Hero Section

The hero is visible without scrolling. It determines whether 80% of visitors continue.

### Hero Requirements
- **Headline**: Primary value proposition in 8 words or fewer
- **Subheadline**: Clarifies who it's for and what they get (1–2 sentences)
- **Primary CTA**: One button, action-oriented, above the fold
- **Visual**: Supporting image, video, or illustration (not decoration)

### Headline Frameworks
- **Outcome headline**: "Get 10x more leads without more ad spend"
- **Problem headline**: "Tired of losing deals to disorganized follow-up?"
- **How-to headline**: "How 5,000 teams ship projects on time"
- **Direct offer**: "Start your free 14-day trial — no credit card required"

### Subheadline Rules
- Expands on the headline, doesn't repeat it
- Includes who the product is for (audience qualifier)
- Maximum 2 lines at desktop width
- Font size: 18–22px, lighter weight than headline

### Hero Visual Strategy
- Product screenshot in device frame: best for software/apps
- Person using the product: best for consumer products
- Abstract/conceptual illustration: use only when product is hard to show
- Avoid stock photo of handshaking businesspeople — erodes trust
- Video loop (muted, auto-play): works for demonstrating motion or process

## Value Proposition Section

Located immediately below the fold, this answers "what exactly do I get?"

### Feature-to-Benefit Translation
Never list features. Always pair with benefits:
- Feature: "Automated follow-up sequences"
- Benefit: "Never let a hot lead go cold again"

Structure each value point as: **[Benefit] — [Feature as proof]**

### Three-Column Icon Grid (most common pattern)
- 3–4 value points
- Icon or small illustration per point
- Bold benefit headline per point (4–6 words)
- 1–2 sentence explanation
- Works at all screen sizes, stacks on mobile

---

## Social Proof

Social proof is not optional. Visitors who encounter it convert at 2–4x higher rates.

### Social Proof Types (by strength)
1. **Case study with specific results**: Strongest — "Acme increased revenue 140% in 90 days"
2. **Named testimonial with photo**: Second — real person with real outcome
3. **Logo wall**: Third — recognizable company names as customers
4. **Review aggregation**: Fourth — "4.8/5 from 2,400 reviews on G2"
5. **Usage numbers**: Fifth — "Trusted by 50,000 teams"

### Testimonial Design Rules
- Full name + title + company (no anonymous testimonials)
- Photo of the person (real photo, not stock)
- Quote that addresses a specific objection or result
- Add star rating if applicable
- 1–3 testimonials above the fold; more in dedicated section below

### Logo Wall Guidelines
- Show logos customers recognize — aspirational + relatable
- Minimum 6, maximum 15 logos for dense grids
- Consistent logo treatment: grayscale or one color, same height
- Label above: "Trusted by teams at..." not "Our customers"

## CTA Hierarchy and Placement

### The Rule of Multiple CTAs
Include the same primary CTA at multiple scroll positions:
- Above the fold (hero)
- After the value proposition (mid-page)
- After social proof
- In the final section (exit CTA)

Never change the CTA copy or action mid-page — this creates confusion. You can change the button label wording slightly but the destination and action must be identical.

### CTA Copy Principles
- Use first person: "Start my free trial" outperforms "Start your free trial"
- Action verb + specific outcome: "Get the template" not "Submit"
- Reduce perceived risk: "No credit card required" below the button
- Urgency (use sparingly): "Limited spots — join now" only if actually true

### CTA Button Design
- Contrasting color that appears nowhere else on the page
- Minimum 44px height, wider than the text by 48px+ padding
- Subtle shadow or hover state communicates clickability
- Ghost/outline button for secondary action alongside primary

---

## Fold Strategy

### What "Above the Fold" Means in Practice
There is no single fold — it varies by screen, browser, and zoom level. Design assuming:
- Mobile: 568–736px visible height
- Desktop: 700–900px visible height
- The hero section should be complete in 700px on desktop

### Scroll Encouragement
If important content is below the fold:
- Subtle downward arrow or scroll indicator
- Hero image that's partially cropped (creates visual tension to scroll)
- Headline that implies there's more ("Here's exactly how it works")

### Content Ordering Principle
Order content by what matters most to a skeptical stranger — not what you find most impressive. Decision makers need context before they care about features.

## Objection Handling

Every visitor has objections. Address them directly rather than hoping they disappear.

### Common Objections and Where to Address Them
- "Is it too expensive?" → Pricing section with value anchoring
- "Is it too complicated?" → Setup timeline or onboarding preview
- "Can I trust this company?" → Team photos, About section, press mentions
- "What if it doesn't work for me?" → Money-back guarantee, free trial, cancel anytime
- "Is it secure?" → Security badges, compliance certifications

### FAQ Section
An FAQ near the CTA handles last-mile objections. Include 5–8 questions derived from actual sales objections. Accordion format preserves visual cleanliness.

## Conversion Optimization Patterns

### Friction Reduction
- Reduce form fields to minimum viable (email only for top-of-funnel)
- Single-step forms outperform multi-step for awareness CTAs
- Multi-step forms outperform single-step for high-intent CTAs (more qualified leads)
- Auto-fill and autofocus the first form field

### Anchoring for Pricing
- Show the higher tier first (left or top)
- Highlight the "recommended" plan with visual treatment
- Show annual savings prominently ("Save 30% annually")
- Show per-user or per-day pricing to reduce sticker shock

### Urgency and Scarcity
- Only use when genuinely true — false urgency destroys trust permanently
- Real urgency: "Enrollment closes Friday" (webinar, cohort)
- Real scarcity: "3 spots left at this price" (limited consulting)
- Never countdown timers that reset on page refresh

## Page Structure Checklist

For a typical product/SaaS landing page, in order:

- [ ] Navigation (minimal — consider removing for pure landing pages)
- [ ] Hero: headline + subheadline + CTA + visual
- [ ] Social proof strip (logos or review aggregate)
- [ ] Value proposition (3–4 benefits with icons)
- [ ] Product walkthrough (screenshots, GIF, or video)
- [ ] Key feature deep-dives (2–3 most important)
- [ ] Testimonials (2–3 detailed quotes)
- [ ] Pricing section
- [ ] FAQ (5–8 real objections)
- [ ] Final CTA section
- [ ] Footer with minimal links

---

## Mobile Design

- Single column layout throughout
- CTA button: full width (minus padding)
- Hero: reduce visual complexity, prioritize copy
- Navigation: hamburger or hidden (reduce exit points)
- Font size: 16px minimum body, 28–36px headlines
- Tap targets: 44x44px minimum for all interactive elements
- Images: lazy load, compressed, proper dimensions

---

## Performance

Page speed is a conversion factor. Each 1-second delay costs ~7% conversions.

- [ ] Largest Contentful Paint under 2.5 seconds
- [ ] Total page weight under 2MB
- [ ] Hero image compressed (WebP preferred)
- [ ] Web fonts: maximum 2 families, subset to used characters
- [ ] No render-blocking scripts in `<head>`
- [ ] CDN for static assets
- [ ] Above-the-fold CSS inlined

## Analytics and Testing Framework

### Baseline Metrics to Track
- Unique visitors
- Scroll depth (25%, 50%, 75%, 100%)
- CTA click rate (clicks / visitors)
- Form submission rate (submissions / CTA clicks)
- Overall conversion rate (conversions / visitors)

### A/B Testing Priority Order
1. Headline copy (highest leverage)
2. CTA button copy
3. Hero image vs. no image
4. Price presentation
5. Form length
6. Page length (long vs. short)

### Testing Rules
- One variable at a time
- Minimum 100 conversions per variant before concluding
- Run for full business cycles (minimum 2 weeks)
- Document everything — variant, hypothesis, result, decision
